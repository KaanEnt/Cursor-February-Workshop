# The VBF Framework: Outside-In Messaging

## Core Principle: Outside-In, Not Inside-Out

You know your product's awesomeness stems from key features. But leading with them in your messaging doesn't capture the awesomeness in your buyers' eyes, because it's not clear how those features might help them.

An inside-out message puts the burden on the reader to figure out what that means, where it could fit in their daily routine, and what problems it might solve. That's too much work for a busy buyer.

**Punchy messaging makes it easier for buyers by aligning with their perspective and going from the outside in.**

### Example: Observability Software

| Approach | Headline |
|----------|----------|
| **VBF (Outside In)** | Resolve issues before customers notice. Stop problems before they start by monitoring your entire stack's performance in one dashboard. |
| **FBV (Inside Out)** | Intuitive dashboard. Our dashboard provides visibility into your entire stack's performance, so you can resolve issues before customers notice. |

Which headline is more compelling? The VBF version leads with a powerful outcome for the buyer, while the other is just describing a dashboard.

## The VBF Components Defined

### Feature (Like a Secret Weapon)

A feature is something special your product has or can do. Think of it like a secret weapon inside your product that makes it powerful.

**Examples:** real-time analytics, custom dashboard, anomaly detection

**Star Wars Analogy:** The Force is like a feature. It's a powerful energy field that binds the galaxy together. But if you haven't seen the movies, that explanation is massively underselling it. That's what happens when you just explain a feature.

### Benefit (Like a Superpower)

A benefit is something new your customer can do, be, or feel, thanks to a feature. Think of it like a new superpower they get when they use your product.

**Examples:**
- Get an instant view across your financials
- Reduce manual work and errors
- Speed up the application process for candidates

**Star Wars Analogy:** The Force gives Luke Skywalker superpowers. He can do backflips with a lightsaber. He can fly a spaceship through a crevice with his eyes shut. He can control people's minds! These are all benefits of the Force. Now anyone can clearly see how powerful the Force is.

### Value Proposition (Like a Big Win)

A value proposition is how multiple benefits add up to a bigger goal your customer is hoping to achieve. Value propositions often connect to larger business goals or big aspirations.

**Example:** The benefit of "having an instant view across financials" could lead to a value proposition of "fuel more growth opportunities with better cash flow."

**Star Wars Analogy:** Luke Skywalker's goal is defeating Darth Vader and bringing peace to the galaxy. All the fancy backflips and lightsaber antics are in service to this bigger, more meaningful goal.

## The VBF Rule Structure

Structure messaging that champions your customers by following the VBF Rule. The letters V-B-F appear in the same order you want to use in your messaging:

1. **Lead with value** (the big win)
2. **Benefits come second** (the superpowers)
3. **Features are last** (the how)

### The Minimalist Framework

```
VALUE PROPOSITION
├── Benefit 1 → Features
├── Benefit 2 → Features
└── Benefit 3 → Features
```

**Why three benefits?** There's magic in the number three. It's big enough to show the range of what's possible with your solution, but still small enough to be digested and remembered. People have a hard time recalling four or more things about your product.

## VBF Comparison: Fancy Blender Example

| VBF (Outside In) | FBV (Inside Out) |
|------------------|------------------|
| **Eat healthier every day** | **Commercial-grade blades** |
| Make vitamin-packed smoothies in a snap with commercial-grade blades. | Our commercial-grade blades make vitamin-packed smoothies quickly, so you can eat healthier. |

The VBF version champions the customer by showing the cool new thing you can do with the blender. The FBV version champions the product by leading with the cool new thing the blades can do.

The VBF version speaks to you on a human level. You can relate to the desire to eat healthier, and you want to do it with a great-tasting smoothie that takes minimal effort to make.

## Simple Is Memorable

Steve Jobs showed up to work in the same outfit for years: black turtleneck, blue jeans, New Balance sneakers. Three simple items, worn every day, created a memorable perception.

Imagine if Jobs had a typical wardrobe with random T-shirts, button-up shirts, formal trousers, hoodies, Nikes. He would have thrown on a different outfit every day, with none of it adding up to a clear style. No one would remember his look.

**The same applies to your messaging.** Keep it simple so people can easily repeat it. The fewer options in your messaging, the more effective it will be.

## Why Not Four or More Benefits?

In Punchy Training Workshops, more than half of leadership teams ask if they can have four or five benefits.

**Cautionary tale:** One VP of marketing asked for four benefits "just this once." A year later, he came back insisting on three: "We tried to do four, but it ended up being too much. We lacked focus and there was too much to juggle. I don't know why, but it just didn't work."

Challenge yourself to get it down to three. You can do it.
