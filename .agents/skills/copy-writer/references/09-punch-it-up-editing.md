# Punch It Up: SMIT and Conciseness

## The Golden Paperclip Problem

In school, there was a contest to fit the most vocabulary words into one sentence. The most flowery, highfalutin sentence always won.

**Example winners:**
- "After four consecutive days of persistent rain, Jenny was reluctant to commence playing outside."
- "Billy had adequate time to contemplate his unique circumstance."

This game set students on the path of long-winded, overly formal writing for years. How you learned to write in school—big words and long sentences—shapes how you write in business.

## Everyone's Dirty Secret

Practically every marketer confesses: "My writing is too long because I struggle to be concise."

**The reassurance:** Concise writing doesn't just happen for the chosen few. It's the result of the editing process. Long-windedness is the starting point for all of us.

**Expect your first draft to be too long.** This chapter gives you techniques to trim it down.

## SMIT: Single Most Important Takeaway

For every message, ask: "If my reader only remembered one thing, what would it be?"

The hard truth: You're lucky if your reader remembers even one thing. Their mind is ruthlessly filtering out excess information. The more you put in front of them, the less they'll remember.

### Example: Enterprise Communication Platform

**Before (too many ideas):**
> Now you can reduce back-to-back meetings by working async across time zones and cross-functional teams. Increase productivity across the business by giving people the time and flexibility to do their best work without interruption. You can also easily capture and share knowledge across teams, for more effective onboarding and training.

**SMIT identified:** "Less meetings for more productivity" — connects to what buyer wants most and what product uniquely delivers.

**After (focused):**
> Now you can reduce back-to-back meetings by working async across time zones and cross-functional teams. Increase productivity across the business by giving people the time and flexibility to do their best work without interruption. ~~You can also easily capture and share knowledge across teams, for more effective onboarding and training.~~

The last sentence was crowding the SMIT and trying to steal the spotlight. It had to go.

### Pro Tip: SMIT Everything

Use SMIT beyond messaging: blog post paragraphs, presentation slides, landing pages, emails to colleagues. It's a simple reminder to focus on what's most important for your reader.

## Short Is Bold

The more concise your message, the bolder it sounds. Every head of marketing wants messaging that exudes the confidence of a clear market leader.

**Long-winded sentences have the opposite effect:** they make you sound shy and unsure.

| Long-winded | Concise & Bold |
|-------------|----------------|
| "Leverage over a dozen data destinations simultaneously" | "Use 12+ destinations at once" |

### Technique 1: Start Sentences with Active Verbs

Starting with a verb makes your reader feel like you're speaking TO them, not about them. It champions what cool new things THEY can do.

**Getting to the point faster:** Don't set the stage with preambles like "our platform is purpose-built to..." That pushes your point to the end—people might not make it that far.

**Before:**
> The platform is designed to enable customers to run analytics on data that is stored in MySQL databases.

**After:**
> Run analytics on data stored in MySQL databases.

### Technique 2: Shorten Sentences

**Research from American Press Institute:**

| Average Sentence Length | Reader Understanding |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| 8 words | 100% |
| 14 words | 90% |
| 43 words | <10% |

Many B2B tech websites have sentences in the 30-40 word range. **No one's understanding it.**

**Edit words like a wedding guest list.** Imagine each word costs $80. Would you invite your annoying second cousin? Look at each word and ask: Does my sentence lose meaning without it? Does it make the sentence better? If no, delete it.

**Before:**
> HR teams can easily create visually appealing career sites that better resonate with job seekers and meet their organizations' brand standards without having to purchase additional products or seek help from IT.

**After:**
> Create visually appealing career sites that meet your brand standards without needing help from IT.

**Rule:** Keep sentences under 20 words.

### Technique 3: Swap Big Words with Little Words

Trade big, fancy words for short, simple ones. Not because your reader isn't smart enough—but because big words slow readers down, like wading through mud.

**Before:**
> The platform is purpose-built to accelerate the identification, diagnosis, and resolution of issues across complex IT environments.

**After:**
> See, find, and fix issues quickly across your IT landscape.

### Punchy Word Swap Reference

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| Accelerate | Speed up |
| Leverage | Use |
| Identify | Find |
| Utilize | Use |
| Implement | Do |
| Enable | Let, allow, help |
| Provide | Give |
| Visualize | See |
| Mitigate | Reduce |
| Facilitate | Improve, ease |

| Alternatively | Or |
| Optimize | Boost, improve, up |

## Make It Spicy

Once you've shrunk to the SMIT, it might feel stripped of personality. This is your chance to spice things up.

### Headline Techniques

**Evoke Emotion:**
Connect to past experiences. Emotion gets attention and motivates change.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Save time with fewer meetings. | Free yourself from back-to-back meetings. |

**Swap in the Unexpected:**
Replace a regular word with an unexpected alternative. These add personality, humanness, and catch the eye.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Brainstorm ideas together. | Riff on ideas together. |

**Rhyming, Repetition, and Alliteration:**
Classic techniques that make messages roll off the tongue with a pleasing, memorable sound.

| Technique | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Rhyming | Delete every spreadsheet. |
| Repetition | Helpful agents, happy customers. |
| Alliteration | Catch customer concerns. |

### Body Copy Techniques

**Paint a Picture (concrete > abstract):**
Sometimes you need MORE words to make things clear. Clear and concise isn't about compressing into one or two words. That's how you end up with fuzzy phrases like "outcome-based strategies."

| Abstract | Concrete (more words, more clear) |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| Tool consolidation | No more juggling multiple tools |
| All-in-one | Everything you need at your fingertips |

**Before:**
> Improve customer communication and payment.

**After:**
> End games of phone tag and make it easy for customers to pay with built-in email, texting, and payment options.

**Show the Pro without the Con:**
Remind readers of their current problem and add emotional response. Key word: **without**.

**Examples:**
- Know where your project stands **without** having to call a status meeting.
- Find what you need **without** searching across tools.
- Understand user behavior **without** drowning in the numbers.

**Mix Up the Pace:**
Vary sentence length to add momentum. Long sentence followed by a short one. Like this. It draws the reader through.

**Before:**
> Get visibility into your current shipping operations and relevant insights into future costs and expenditures. The right software solution and the right experts will save your business time and money.

**After:**
> See where your shipping costs are today—and where they'll be tomorrow—in a snap. No more surprises.

**Drop Some Parentheses:**
Catch the eye as a disruption. Fun and playful way to show brand personality. Gives the vibe of a friend whispering an aside (as though you're getting the inside scoop).

**Example:**
> Stop looking for a needle in the haystack of online leads. Make people feel special with personalized campaigns (and prove you're an actual human) so you get more responses, applications, and appointments.

## A Note on Translation

Can you spice up messaging that will be translated?

**Yes—as long as you avoid colloquialisms** (e.g., "nip issues in the bud") that don't translate.

Always aim for simple and concise language. Shorter words and sentences will set your messaging up to be clearly translated for global audiences.

## The Editing Reality

In Punchy Training Workshops, marketers spend several hours writing and refining a few ultra-clear messages. At the end: "Wow! I forgot how long it takes to write really good messaging."

**Writing is more than slapping words on a page. It's a form of thinking.** You sharpen your thoughts and ideas through the writing process. At the end, you have clarity that simply wasn't there before.

That clarity is critical to building a winning perception. Give yourself the time and space to put all you've got into your messaging.
