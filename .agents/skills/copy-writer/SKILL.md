# Punchy Copy Writer

Transform customer call transcripts into punchy website copy using Emma Stratton's methodology from "Make It Punchy." This skill processes customer interviews, discovery calls, and transcripts to extract authentic customer language and generate compelling website messaging.

## Core Philosophy

**Punchy messaging is customer-centric, not product-centric.** The Cambridge Dictionary defines "punchy" as "expressing something effectively and with power, often using fewer and shorter words." Messaging creates an impression of your product. If your messaging is dense and complicated, people assume your product is too.

**Key Insight**: Messaging isn't about your product. It's about the cool new things your buyer will be able to do, be, and feel, thanks to your product.

## When to Use This Skill

Use when you need to:
- Process customer call transcripts to extract pains, desires, and authentic language
- Generate website copy (homepage, product pages, landing pages)
- Write value propositions and benefit statements
- Transform inside-out (feature-focused) messaging to outside-in (customer-focused) messaging
- Edit existing copy to remove jargon and add punch

## The VBF Framework (Core Structure)

Every piece of messaging follows VBF order: **Value proposition first, Benefits second, Features last.**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│           VALUE PROPOSITION                  │
│     (The Big Win for the Customer)          │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Benefit 1    │  Benefit 2    │  Benefit 3  │
│  (Superpower) │  (Superpower) │  (Superpower)│
├───────────────┼───────────────┼─────────────┤
│  Features     │  Features     │  Features   │
│  (How)        │  (How)        │  (How)      │
└───────────────┴───────────────┴─────────────┘
```

### What Each Component Does

| Component | Definition | Star Wars Analogy |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Feature** | Something special your product has or can do | The Force (a powerful energy field) |
| **Benefit** | New thing customer can DO, BE, or FEEL | Luke's superpowers (backflips, mind control, flying blind) |
| **Value Proposition** | How benefits add up to a bigger goal | Defeat Darth Vader and restore peace to the galaxy |

### The VBF Rule in Action

**Outside-In (VBF - Good):**
> Eat healthier every day. Make vitamin-packed smoothies in a snap with commercial-grade blades.

**Inside-Out (FBV - Bad):**
> Commercial-grade blades. Our commercial-grade blades make vitamin-packed smoothies quickly, so you can eat healthier.

## Process: Transcript to Website Copy

### Step 1: Extract Customer Intelligence

When processing a transcript, extract and organize:

#### A+ Customer Profile
- Who is the happiest customer type?
- Who gets the most value from the product?
- Who are the super users?
- How tech-savvy are they?
- How aware are they of solutions like this?

#### Urgent Pains (BBQ-Friendly Language)
Ask: "Would they say this to a friend at a BBQ while drinking a beer?"

| Un-BBQ-Friendly | BBQ-Friendly |
|-----------------|--------------|
| "Need to streamline communications" | "Need to get the constant calls, texts, and emails under control" |
| "Boost developer productivity" | "Let devs focus on building cool stuff instead of boring manual tasks" |
| "Foster continuous engagement" | "Keep everyone on the team involved" |

**Questions to identify pains:**
- What are their smack-in-the-face daily challenges?
- What wakes them up at 2 a.m. in a cold sweat?
- What are the life impacts of work problems?

#### Deep-Down Desires
- What are their goals on the horizon?
- What personal motivations drive them?
- How would they like to change for the better?

#### Exact Customer Language
Capture phrases verbatim. The best messaging often comes directly from customer quotes:
- "A system they didn't have to hand-hold or babysit"
- "Finally get their nights and weekends back"
- "Take the fear out of checking your account balance"

### Step 2: Identify Product's Best Qualities

**Best qualities** are unique features that directly benefit customers. NOT:
- Under-the-hood technical things that don't connect to benefits
- Unsubstantiated claims like "best customer experience"

**Questions:**
- What consistently makes customers go "Wow!"?
- What do customers routinely rave about?
- What makes you different from competitors?

### Step 3: Connect Pains to Qualities (The "So What?" Game)

For each product quality, keep asking "So what?" until you reach value:

```
Feature: AI fraud detection
↓ So what?
You can detect fraud faster
↓ So what?
You can save time detecting fraud
↓ So what?
You can detect fraud that manual reviews can't find
↓ So what?
You save the headaches of fraud and credit losses
↓ So what?
You reduce risk
↓ So what?
You increase company revenue
```

**Star the benefits** (superpowers: do, be, feel)
**Underline value propositions** (big wins)

### Step 4: Find the Right Altitude

Match message specificity to customer knowledge:

| Customer Type | Altitude | Example |
|---------------|----------|---------|
| Less aware, less technical | Broader, high-level outcomes | "Always know where every project stands" |
| More aware, more technical | More specific, differentiated | "Maintain reliable, secure infrastructure to avoid disruptions" |

### Step 5: Stake Your Claim (Unique Value)

Your unique value is the ONE thing you want to be known for. Find it through three lenses:

**Your Company's Perspective:**
- What's at the core of your product vision?
- What makes your approach unique?

**Your Customers' Perspective:**
- What do they routinely rave about?
- What do they describe as the main benefit?

**Your Competitors' Perspective:**
- What aren't they talking about?
- Where is there white space?

**Avoid brown shaved ice**: Don't mix too many flavors. Stand for ONE thing.

## Writing the Copy

### Value Proposition Formulas

**Formula 1: The Command One**
> "Now you can [big win]."

Examples:
- Now you can build in-demand skills at your own pace.
- Now you can predict and prevent production losses.
- Now you can get complex work under control.

**Formula 2: The Aspirational One**
> "Imagine if you could [aspiration]."

Examples:
- Make every customer interaction count.
- Accomplish more together, faster.
- Secure web apps with confidence, not complexity.

**Formula 3: The Ultimate One**
> "Ultimately, you will be able to [larger business goal]."

Examples:
- Create a foundation for sustainable growth.
- Simplify cloud complexity.
- Build a culture of inclusivity.

### Value Proposition Checklist

| Criteria | Test |
|----------|------|
| **Clarity** | Can a sixth grader understand it? Can you read it once and get it? |
| **Uniqueness** | Does it connect to your unique value? Do competitors claim this? |
| **Truth** | Can we back this up with numbers or customer proof? |

### Benefit Writing Formulas

**The Big Picture:**
> "[Describe benefit], so you can [larger goal or outcome]."

Example: Resolve issues quickly and accurately the first time, so your customers become even bigger fans of your brand.

**Before and After:**
> "Stop [before] and start [after]."

Example: Stop bouncing from system to system and feeling bonkers along the way. In your workspace, your team can see everything at once.

**Benefit Progression:**
> "You can [superpower 1], [superpower 2], and [superpower 3]."

Example: Locate every threat, figure out what the attackers are after, and keep your data and systems safe for good.

### Benefit Checklist

| Criteria | Test |
|----------|------|
| **Empowerment** | Does it elevate the customer and show what cool new things THEY can do? |
| **Directness** | Does it get straight to the point without preambles? |
| **Concreteness** | Can you picture it in your mind? Is it specific, not abstract? |

## Editing: Ditch the Jargon

### The Four Flavors of Jargon

| Type | Examples | Problem |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Corporate-Speak** | leverage, synergy, bandwidth, agility | Sounds robotic, adds no clarity |
| **Buzzwords/Hype** | transform, revolutionize, supercharge, game-changing | Lost all meaning, glorifies product not customer |
| **Internal Lingo** | end-to-end ecosystem, composable automation | Customers aren't in your head |
| **Deep Tech Talk** | augmented analytics, ML-defined data abstraction | Only experts understand |

### The "Is It Jargon?" Test

Ask:
1. Will it be universally understood by your audience?
2. Is it meaningful to your audience?

If no to either, translate it.

### De-Jargon Process

1. **Hunt for jargon** in your messaging
2. **Translate what it actually means** to the buyer
3. **Describe what it looks like** through their eyes

**Before:** Achieve operational excellence
**After:** Deliver consistent service

**Before:** Unleash the power of 5G with best-of-breed, multigenerational network infrastructure
**After:** Move fast with 5G while preserving the value of your 3G and 4G networks

## Editing: Make It Punchy (SMIT + Conciseness)

### SMIT: Single Most Important Takeaway

For every message, ask: "If my reader only remembered one thing, what would it be?"

Remove everything that distracts from the SMIT.

**Before (too many ideas):**
> Now you can reduce back-to-back meetings by working async across time zones and cross-functional teams. Increase productivity across the business by giving people the time and flexibility to do their best work without interruption. You can also easily capture and share knowledge across teams, for more effective onboarding and training.

**After (focused on SMIT):**
> Now you can reduce back-to-back meetings by working async across time zones and cross-functional teams. Increase productivity by giving people the time and flexibility to do their best work without interruption.

### Short Is Bold

| Technique | How To | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Start with active verbs** | Begin sentences with verbs | "Run analytics on data" not "The platform is designed to enable customers to run analytics" |
| **Shorten sentences** | Keep under 20 words | Edit like a wedding guest list: does each word earn its $80 seat? |
| **Swap big words** | Use simple alternatives | "Use" not "utilize", "Find" not "identify", "Do" not "implement" |

### Word Swap Reference

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| Accelerate | Speed up |
| Leverage | Use |
| Identify | Find |
| Utilize | Use |
| Implement | Do |
| Enable | Let, allow, help |
| Provide | Give |
| Visualize | See |
| Mitigate | Reduce |
| Facilitate | Improve, ease |
| Ensure | Make sure |
| Optimize | Boost, improve |
| Eliminate | End |

## Adding Spice

### Headline Techniques

**Evoke Emotion:**
> Before: Save time with fewer meetings.
> After: Free yourself from back-to-back meetings.

**Swap in the Unexpected:**
> Before: Brainstorm ideas together.
> After: Riff on ideas together.

**Rhyming, Repetition, Alliteration:**
- Rhyming: Delete every spreadsheet.
- Repetition: Helpful agents, happy customers.
- Alliteration: Catch customer concerns.

### Body Copy Techniques

**Paint a Picture (concrete > abstract):**
> Before: tool consolidation
> After: no more juggling multiple tools

**Show the Pro without the Con:**
> Know where your project stands without having to call a status meeting.

**Mix Up the Pace:**
> See where your shipping costs are today. And where they'll be tomorrow. In a snap. No more surprises.

**Drop Parentheses for Asides:**
> Make people feel special with personalized campaigns (and prove you're an actual human) so you get more responses.

## The Punchy Story Framework

For major messaging (homepage, company positioning), craft a story:

### 5 Phases

1. **The Shift** - What's changing in the customer's world that forces them to act?
2. **The Villain** - What's the face of the core problem? (object, approach, concept, person)
3. **Three Obstacles** - What subproblems does the villain create? (maps to your 3 benefits)
4. **The Dream** - What do they wish things could be like? (tangible outcome + feeling)
5. **The Solution** - How does your product help them achieve the dream?

### Example Structure (Slack)

1. **Shift:** Tech teams moving faster, communication can't keep pace
2. **Villain:** Email
3. **Obstacles:** Can't find what you need / Missing out on siloed communication / Replies taking too long
4. **Dream:** Being more productive without wasting time on admin
5. **Solution:** One place for real-time messaging, archive, and search

## Output Format

When generating website copy, deliver:

### 1. Customer Intelligence Summary
```
A+ CUSTOMER: [Description]
TECH-SAVVINESS: [Low/Medium/High]
AWARENESS LEVEL: [New to category / Familiar / Expert]

URGENT PAINS (BBQ-friendly):
- [Pain 1 in customer language]
- [Pain 2 in customer language]
- [Pain 3 in customer language]

DEEP DESIRES:
- [Desire 1]
- [Desire 2]

AUTHENTIC LANGUAGE (direct quotes):
- "[Quote 1]"
- "[Quote 2]"
```

### 2. VBF Messaging Framework
```
VALUE PROPOSITION: [One sentence]

BENEFIT 1: [Headline]
[1-3 sentence supporting message]
Features: [bullet list]

BENEFIT 2: [Headline]
[1-3 sentence supporting message]
Features: [bullet list]

BENEFIT 3: [Headline]
[1-3 sentence supporting message]
Features: [bullet list]
```

### 3. Punchy Story (Optional, for major messaging)
```
THE SHIFT: [1-2 sentences]
THE VILLAIN: [Named enemy]
OBSTACLES: [3 bullet points]
THE DREAM: [Tangible outcome + feeling]
THE SOLUTION: [How product enables the dream]
```

### 4. Website Copy Drafts
```
HOMEPAGE HERO
Headline: [Value proposition]
Subhead: [Supporting benefit or context]
CTA: [Action button text]

BENEFIT SECTION 1
Headline: [Benefit 1]
Body: [Supporting copy]

BENEFIT SECTION 2
Headline: [Benefit 2]
Body: [Supporting copy]

BENEFIT SECTION 3
Headline: [Benefit 3]
Body: [Supporting copy]
```

## Quality Checklist

Before delivering copy, verify:

- [ ] **Customer-centric**: Is the customer the hero, not the product?
- [ ] **VBF order**: Value first, then benefits, features last?
- [ ] **BBQ test**: Would customers say this to a friend?
- [ ] **Jargon-free**: No corporate-speak, buzzwords, or internal lingo?
- [ ] **SMIT clear**: Is the single most important takeaway obvious?
- [ ] **Under 20 words**: Are sentences short and punchy?
- [ ] **Active verbs**: Do sentences start with verbs?
- [ ] **Concrete**: Can you picture it in your mind?
- [ ] **Unique**: Does it differentiate from competitors?
- [ ] **True**: Can we back this up?
