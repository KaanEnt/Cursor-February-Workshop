# Ogilvy's Power Words Lexicon

> "Certain words and phrases have been tested and proven to increase effectiveness." — David Ogilvy

## The Psychology of Power Words

Power words tap into deep psychological triggers:
- **Curiosity** - Makes readers need to know more
- **Greed/Self-interest** - Promises personal benefit
- **Fear** - Highlights risk of inaction
- **Novelty** - Appeals to desire for the new

---

## Power Words by Category

### News & Novelty

These words signal something fresh and worth attention:

| Word | Why It Works |
|------|--------------|
| **New** | Triggers novelty-seeking behavior |
| **Now** | Creates immediacy |
| **Announcing** | Signals importance |
| **Introducing** | Frames as a debut |
| **Finally** | Implies long-awaited solution |
| **At Last** | Suggests relief after waiting |
| **Suddenly** | Creates dramatic interest |
| **Startling** | Promises surprise |

### Utility & How-To

These words promise practical value:

| Word | Why It Works |
|------|--------------|
| **How to** | Among highest-performing openers |
| **Why** | Promises explanation |
| **Which** | Implies selection help |
| **This** | Creates specificity |
| **Advice** | Positions as helpful guidance |
| **Facts** | Promises substance over fluff |
| **Tips** | Suggests actionable takeaways |
| **Secrets** | Implies exclusive knowledge |

### Value & Benefit

These words promise something valuable to the reader:

| Word | Why It Works |
|------|--------------|
| **Free** | Most powerful word in advertising |
| **Save** | Appeals to loss aversion |
| **Easy** | Reduces perceived effort |
| **Quick** | Appeals to time-consciousness |
| **Guaranteed** | Removes risk |
| **Proven** | Provides social proof |
| **Results** | Promises outcomes |
| **Bargain** | Signals value |

### Urgency & Scarcity

These words motivate immediate action:

| Word | Why It Works |
|------|--------------|
| **Hurry** | Creates time pressure |
| **Last Chance** | Triggers loss aversion |
| **Only** | Implies scarcity |
| **Today** | Limits timeframe |
| **Limited** | Suggests exclusivity |
| **Deadline** | Creates concrete urgency |

### Emotion & Connection

These words create emotional resonance:

| Word | Why It Works |
|------|--------------|
| **You/Your** | Makes it personal |

These words engage rational thinking:

| Word | Why It Works |

## Words to Avoid

### Empty Superlatives

These words have lost all meaning through overuse:

- Best
- Amazing
- Revolutionary
- Game-changing
- World-class
- Premium
- Superior

**The problem:** Everyone claims these. They provide no differentiation.

### Corporate Jargon

These words sound impressive but say nothing:

- Synergy
- Leverage (as a verb)
- Paradigm
- Optimize
- Utilize
- Facilitate
- Implement
- End-to-end
- Best-of-breed
- Cutting-edge

### Meaningless Modifiers

- Very
- Really
- Extremely
- Incredibly
- Absolutely

**Ogilvy's advice:** If you need a modifier to make the word strong enough, find a stronger word.

## The Quotation Mark Effect

Headlines in quotation marks increase recall by 28%.

**Why:** They imply someone is speaking, adding a human element.

**Examples:**
- "I never thought I'd enjoy exercising until..."
- "The best investment I ever made"
- "Finally, a solution that actually works"

## Combining Power Words

The most effective headlines combine multiple triggers:

### News + Benefit
"**New** way to **save** $500 on your energy bill"

### How-To + Specificity
"**How to** write headlines that get 47% more clicks"

### Urgency + Value
"**Last chance** to get **free** shipping **today**"

### Curiosity + Benefit
"The **secret** to **doubling** your productivity"

## Testing Your Word Choices

### The Substitution Test

Take your headline and try substituting power words:
- Replace "good" with "proven"
- Replace "learn" with "discover"
- Replace "get" with "claim"

### The Specificity Test

Can you make vague words specific?
- "Save money" → "Save $500"
- "Quick results" → "Results in 7 days"
- "Many customers" → "47,000 customers"

## Power Words in Email Subject Lines

Given the 40-60 character constraint, power words must be:

1. **Front-loaded** - Put the power word early before cutoff
2. **Singular** - One strong word beats multiple weak ones
3. **Relevant** - Match the power word to what you're actually offering

**Examples:**
- "**New:** 3 features you asked for"
- "**How to** fix your biggest sales problem"
- "**Free** guide: Headlines that convert"
- "**Finally:** The tool you've been waiting for"
